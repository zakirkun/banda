## Origin of the Name

**Banda** takes its name from the **Banda Islands (Kepulauan Banda)** in Maluku, Indonesia.

For centuries, Banda was:

* The heart of the global spice trade (nutmeg and mace)
* A crossroads of cultures, ideas, and conflict
* A place of exile for independence figures such as **Mohammad Hatta**
* A region rich in **history, resilience, and natural beauty**
* Home to living traditions like **Cakalele dance** and **Sasi**, a local wisdom of balance and restraint

Banda is not just a place â€”
it is a story of **value**, **control**, **consequence**, and **endurance**.

This framework carries that meaning forward.

## Banda Is Not a Trend

Banda is not built to follow trends.
It does not chase abstractions, hype cycles, or fashionable complexity.

Just as Banda once shaped the world quietly through something as simple as a spice,
**Banda the framework believes that small, well-crafted primitives can shape great systems.**

## The Core Philosophy

### 1. Simplicity Is Strength

Banda believes that:

* Directness beats indirection
* Fewer layers mean fewer lies
* Code should reveal intent, not hide it

There is no Virtual DOM.
There is no JSX.
There is no illusion.

Only:

* The DOM
* Clear data flow
* Explicit behavior

What you write is what runs.

---

### 2. Control Over Convenience

Banda values **control** over forced convenience.

You decide:

* How elements are created
* How state flows
* How updates propagate
* How styles are applied

Banda does not guess.
Banda does not auto-magically rewire your code.
Banda respects the developer's awareness.

This mirrors **Banda's history**, where control over a small thing (nutmeg) changed empires.

---

### 3. Minimal Primitives, Infinite Composition

Banda provides **primitives**, not solutions.

Like:

* `defineComponent`
* `createSignal`
* Design tokens
* DOM elements

From these small parts, you compose systems as large as you need â€”
without ever losing understanding of how they work.

Just as **Banda Neira** is small yet historically immense,
Banda's surface area is small, but its potential is vast.

---

### 4. Ownership Over Abstraction

Banda avoids abstraction that removes ownership.

If something exists:

* You can read it
* You can debug it
* You can replace it

There are no black boxes.
There are no hidden schedulers.
There are no invisible render cycles.

This is a framework for engineers who want to **own their UI**, not negotiate with it.

---

### 5. Design as a System, Not Decoration

Banda treats design as **structure**, not cosmetics.

Spacing, color, motion, and typography are:

* Tokenized
* Predictable
* Themeable
* Intentional

This reflects **local wisdom like Sasi** â€”
a system of rules that protects balance rather than exploiting resources.

Banda's design system is about restraint, consistency, and long-term sustainability.

---

### 6. Accessibility Is a Responsibility

Banda believes accessibility is not optional.

Just as Banda's culture values community and shared space,
Banda the framework ensures that interfaces are:

* Keyboard accessible
* Screen-reader friendly
* Motion-aware
* Inclusive by default

Accessibility is not a plugin.
It is part of the foundation.

---

### 7. Performance Through Honesty

Banda does not optimize by trickery.
It optimizes by **clarity**.

* Fine-grained updates
* Minimal DOM writes
* No unnecessary observers
* No wasted renders

Performance is a natural result of understanding â€”
not a side effect of complexity.
